Io-1

Io-1, the first thruster developed by SUEP and the foundational technology from which all of our thrusters have been developed. This thruster was the first to be developed by undergraduate students as part of a society project, and it has taught us valuable skills providing us with knowledge we are using to develop our next thrusters. Io-1 was tested in February 2025 and was successfully ignited as shown below. It was operating on Krypton gas at the David Fearne Electric Propulsion Laboratory, at the University of Southampton. The thruster was unable to operate steadily, and so we were unable to acquire hard data points. Despite this, we can still infer certain aspects of its performance. Based on the prior magnetic field tests, it is shown that the magnetic field is lop-sided, resulting in a non-negligible amount of the magnetic field being non-radial. This resulted in the plume being quite divergent which can be seen in the image below and likely resulted in many ions having non-axial velocity components, not contributing to thrust.
